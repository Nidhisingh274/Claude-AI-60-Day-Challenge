### 📈 Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
* Deep pipeline architecture — from RAG retrieval to agent tool-calling

* Systematic execution — ships consistently, not sporadically
* Full observability mindset — LangSmith monitoring baked in from day one

### 🧠 Learning & Decision-Making Style
Empirical and systematic. She learns by building, not reading. A new framework gets stress-tested in a real project within 48 hours of discovery. Decisions are data-anchored — ambiguity gets resolved by running experiments, not asking for consensus. High-stakes calls (architecture, model selection, deployment strategy) are made like clinical decisions: gather signal, weigh tradeoffs, execute with precision, iterate fast.

**Traits:** *Build-to-learn · Experiment-first · Data-anchored · Decisive · Iterative*

### 🛤️ Best Future Career Paths

* **Senior AI/ML Engineer → Staff AI Engineer:** Natural progression. Leads cross-functional AI pipelines, owns agent infrastructure from design to production. Sets the technical bar for engineering teams.
* **AI Solutions Architect:** Translates business problems into agentic system designs. Sits at the intersection of ML engineering and enterprise strategy — a role her healthcare background makes uniquely credible in regulated industries.

### 🌟 What makes her different from most AI users
Most people use AI as a tool. She builds the tools. The average AI practitioner works within frameworks — she builds on top of them, extending LangChain, designing custom graph states, and wiring monitoring into LangSmith before most engineers even think about observability. Add a clinical data background that trained her to treat every output as something that could carry real-world consequences — and you get an engineer who engineers with accountability baked in by default.

**Differentiators:** *Builder, not user · Healthcare precision instinct · Observability-native · Domain + tech convergence*
